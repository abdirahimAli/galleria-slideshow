Girl with a Pearl Earring

Johannes Vermeer

1665

The painting is a tronie, the Dutch 17th-century
description of a 'head' that was not meant to be
a portrait. It depicts a European girl wearing an
exotic dress, an oriental turban, and what was
thought to be a very large pearl as an earring. In
2014, Dutch astrophysicist Vincent Icke raised
doubts about the material of the earring and
argued that it looks more like polished tin than
pearl on the grounds of the specular reflection,
the pear shape and the large size of the earring.
