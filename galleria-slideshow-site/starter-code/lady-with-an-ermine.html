The Lady with an Ermine (Italian: Dama con
l'ermellino [ˈdaːma kon lermelˈliːno]; Polish: Dama z gronostajem)
is a portrait painting widely attributed to the Italian Renaissance
artist Leonardo da Vinci. Dated to c. 1489–1491, the work is
painted in oils on a panel of walnut wood. Its subject is Cecilia
Gallerani, a mistress of Ludovico Sforza ("Il Moro"), Duke of
Milan; Leonardo was painter to the Sforza court at the
time of its execution. It is one of only four
surviving portraits of women painted by
Leonardo, the others being Ginevra de' Benci,
La Belle Ferronnière and the Mona Lisa.
